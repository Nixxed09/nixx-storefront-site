# Nix Business Plan

## Best Starting Business
Start with **Dummy 13 story-driven 3D printed figures**.

Why this should be the first focus:
- It uses what Nix already has: 3D prints, photos, character ideas, and a website.
- It can make money before games become polished enough to sell.
- It teaches internet marketing through real offers, photos, product pages, bundles, emails, and launch posts.
- It connects to Nix Games instead of competing with it.

## Positioning
Nix is not selling random 3D prints. He is building a kid-created character universe.

Core offer:
> Collectible Dummy 13 heroes with gear, origin stories, and custom print options.

## Product Ladder
| Level | Product | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Free | Nix Games | attention, trust, proof he builds |
| $6-$12 | gear packs, small accessories | easy first purchase |
| $29.99 | single hero figure | main product |
| $79.99 | starter squad bundle | giftable bundle |
| Custom quote | custom hero request | higher-value orders |
| Future | mini storybook or QR story | adds value and collectibility |

## Should Nix Build Games?
Yes, but games should be the long-term brand engine, not the first money engine.

Best split:
- **70% toys/business/marketing** until sales start.
- **30% games** to keep building the universe and traffic source.

Games are hard to monetize quickly. 3D printed figures can earn sooner, while games make the story bigger.

## First Internet Marketing Lessons
Teach Nix these in order:
1. One clear offer beats many scattered ideas.
2. A product page needs photo, price, story, safety note, and call to action.
3. A bundle is easier to sell as a gift than a random single item.
4. Every post needs one job: show, teach, launch, or ask.
5. Track clicks and inquiries before worrying about followers.

## 30-Day Execution Plan
### Week 1: Offer
- Pick 3 real heroes.
- Name them.
- Write motto, class, origin, gear, and lesson.
- Confirm price and print time.

### Week 2: Content
- Photograph each hero from front, side, detail, and group view.
- Make one product page per hero.
- Record short maker clips: printing, assembling, posing.

### Week 3: Launch
- Post one hero per day.
- Send the site to friends/family/local parents.
- Ask for inquiries, not full checkout yet.

### Week 4: Learn
- Count visits, product clicks, inquiries, and questions.
- Improve names, photos, prices, and bundles based on what people ask.

## Next Site Features
- Product detail pages. Done.
- Inquiry form instead of mailto.
- Story download/QR per hero.
- Parent-approved policy page.
- Basic analytics events for product page views and inquiry starts.
